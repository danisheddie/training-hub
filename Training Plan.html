Tuesday Sessions

Indoor Trainer Workouts

Every Tuesday on the trainer. These sessions build your 32 km/h race pace bike leg.

BASE-1 — Steady Endurance

Weeks 1, 2, 3 · 60–70 min total

▸ 10 min — Warm-up Z1 → Z2 (easy spin, find your legs)
▸ 40–50 min — Steady Z2 (conversational pace, full sentences)
▸ 10 min — Cool-down Z1

Focus on pedal stroke smoothness. Cadence: 88–92 rpm. Should never creep above Z2 — if it does, ease off. These feel "too easy." That's correct.

BASE-2 — Endurance + Cadence Drills

Weeks 5, 6, 7 · 75–80 min total

▸ 10 min — Warm-up Z1 → Z2
▸ 5 min — HIGH CADENCE DRILL @ 100+ rpm, Z2
▸ 5 min — Normal cadence Z2 (recover)
▸ 5 min — HIGH CADENCE DRILL @ 100+ rpm, Z2
▸ 5 min — Normal cadence Z2 (recover)
▸ 25–30 min — Steady Z2
▸ 10 min — Cool-down Z1

Fast legs off the bike are trained, not natural. Spinning at 100+ rpm now means your legs switch to run mode faster on race day.

BASE-3 — Recovery Spin

Weeks 4, 8 (Recovery weeks) · 45–50 min

▸ 10 min — Warm-up Z1
▸ 30 min — Easy Z1–Z2 (easy gear, no effort, conversational)
▸ 10 min — Cool-down Z1

This is not a junk session. Active recovery accelerates adaptation. Go easy and mean it.

BUILD-1 — Tempo Blocks

Weeks 9, 10 · 75 min total

▸ 15 min — Warm-up Z1→Z2 (include 3×30s spin-ups to 100 rpm)
▸ 10 min — TEMPO @ Z3 (comfortably hard)
▸ 5 min — Easy Z1–Z2
▸ 10 min — TEMPO @ Z3
▸ 15 min — Cool-down Z1–Z2

Z3 should be genuinely challenging. You can speak but choose not to. If chatting easily, go harder.

Execution

Race Strategy

Discipline-by-discipline execution plan for November 1st.

🏊 Swim

  • Seed yourself wide if unsure of pace — avoid the washing machine start
  • Sight every 8–10 strokes — Kenting ocean may have chop
  • First 300m will be chaotic. Stay calm. Find space.
  • Wetsuit check: water temp ~26–27°C — verify rules close to race day
  • Practise open water sighting from Week 14 in coaching sessions

🚴 Bike

Km 0–15Settle in. HR in Z2–Z3. Hold back.
Km 15–60Race pace. 31–33 km/h. Fuel every 20–25 min.
Km 60–80Where most athletes blow up. Stay disciplined.
Km 80–90Push. You've earned it. Run is coming.
  • Rolling hills — Kenting is not flat. Stand on steep climbs to save legs.
  • Wind possible on exposed coastal sections

🏃 Run

Km 0–56:10–6:20/km — conservative, legs adjusting
Km 5–155:55–6:00/km — race pace locked in
Km 15–215:45–5:55/km — push if feeling good
  • Walk aid stations — costs 15 secs, saves core temp
  • Pour cold water over yourself at EVERY aid station
  • 28–32°C in Kenting. Heat management wins this leg.

Fuel

Race Day Nutrition

Test everything in training — especially in the Race Simulation (Week 20).

Night Before

  • Dinner:Pasta or rice with lean protein
  • Avoid:Heavy sauces, new foods, high fibre
  • Hydrate:2–3 litres throughout the day
  • Sleep:8+ hours — your secret weapon

Race Morning

  • 3 hrs before:Rice cakes / banana + PB / oats
  • Coffee:Only if you normally drink it
  • Fluid:500ml water + electrolytes
  • 30 min out:1 gel + water

🚴 Bike (~2:48)

  • Carbs/hr:60–80g (~280 cal/hr)
  • Every 20–25 min:1 gel or half bar + water
  • Every 30 min:300–400ml electrolyte/water
  • Total:2 full bottles + aid station water

🏃 Run (~2:07)

  • Every 30–35 min:1 gel (2–3 total)
  • Every aid station:Water or cola
  • Walk aid stations:Pour water on neck
  • Avoid:Anything you haven't tested
🌡 Kenting Heat Protocol November temps: 28–32°C, humid. Add 10–15 min to targets vs cooler conditions. Use electrolyte tablets — hyponatremia risk is real when drinking only water in heat. Ice towel on neck at T2. Pre-cool in transition if possible.
