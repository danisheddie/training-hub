# ⚡ WHY THE TRAINER MATTERS

The bike leg is **2:48** of your race — your single longest discipline. A 1 km/h increase in average speed saves you ~5 minutes. These Tuesday sessions are where that speed is built.

**Trainer advantages vs outdoor:**
- Zero coasting — every minute is working time
- Precise zone control — no traffic, no downhills
- Consistent effort regardless of weather
- Safer intervals at high intensity

> 🎯 **Season goal:** Build from casual 1–2x/week riding to holding 32 km/h comfortably for 90km.

---

# 🔧 SETUP

**Before every session:**
- [ ] Fan pointed at you — overheating indoors kills performance and is dangerous
- [ ] 1–2 full water bottles within reach
- [ ] Towel on the bike — sweat is brutal indoors
- [ ] Nutrition within reach for sessions over 75 min

**Smart trainer (ERG mode):** Set to target wattage — trainer holds it automatically. Great for interval precision.
**Basic trainer / no power:** Use HR and RPE (feel) — equally effective, just requires more focus.

**Target cadence:** 85–95 rpm throughout. Higher cadence = more efficient pedalling = better run off the bike.

---

# 💓 ZONE REFERENCE

| Zone | Name | Power (% FTP) | HR (% Max) | RPE | Sound Test |
|------|------|--------------|-----------|-----|------------|
| Z1 | 🟦 Recovery | < 55% | < 68% | 1–2 | Singing comfortably |
| Z2 | 🟩 Aerobic | 56–75% | 68–75% | 3–4 | Full sentences, easy |
| Z3 | 🟨 Tempo | 76–87% | 76–82% | 5–6 | Short sentences only |
| Z4 | 🟧 Threshold | 88–95% | 83–89% | 7–8 | One or two words |
| Z5 | 🟥 VO2 Max | > 106% | 90%+ | 9–10 | Grunts only |

> 💡 **FTP Test:** 20-min all-out effort × 0.95 = your FTP. Retest every 6–8 weeks to watch fitness gains. First test in Week 3 or 4.

# 🔵 PHASE 1: BASE WORKOUTS
## Weeks 1–8 · The Foundation

> *All Z2. All the time. Resist the urge to go harder.*

These sessions feel almost embarrassingly easy. Good. You're building the aerobic infrastructure that will power you through 90km in November.

---

## BASE-1 — Steady Endurance
**Weeks 1, 2, 3 · Duration: 60–70 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2 (spin easy, find your legs)
  ▸ 40–50 min  ── Steady Z2 (conversational pace, full sentences)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1

  Target cadence: 88–92 rpm throughout
  HR: Should never creep above Z2 — if it does, ease off
```

**What to focus on:** Pedal stroke smoothness. Imagine scraping mud off the bottom of your shoe at the bottom of each stroke.

---

## BASE-2 — Endurance + Cadence Drills
**Weeks 5, 6, 7 · Duration: 75–80 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2
  ▸  5 min  ──  🔄 HIGH CADENCE DRILL @ 100+ rpm, Z2
  ▸  5 min  ──  Normal cadence Z2 (recover)
  ▸  5 min  ──  🔄 HIGH CADENCE DRILL @ 100+ rpm, Z2
  ▸  5 min  ──  Normal cadence Z2 (recover)
  ▸ 25–30 min  ── Steady Z2
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1
```

**Why high cadence?** Fast legs off the bike are trained, not natural. Spinning at 100+ rpm now means your legs switch to run mode faster on race day.

---

## BASE-3 — Recovery Spin
**Weeks 4, 8 (Recovery Weeks) · Duration: 45–50 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1
  ▸ 30 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2 (conversational, easy gear, no effort)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1
```

**This is not a junk session.** Active recovery accelerates adaptation. Go easy and mean it.

# 🟡 PHASE 2: BUILD WORKOUTS
## Weeks 9–16 · The Forge

> *Welcome to the uncomfortable. This is where fitness is made.*

---

## BUILD-1 — Tempo Blocks
**Weeks 9, 10 · Duration: 75 min**

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2 (include 3x30s spin-ups to 100 rpm)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3 (comfortably hard, sentences only)
  ▸  5 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2 (recover fully)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Cool-down Z1–Z2
```

**Target feel:** Z3 should be genuinely challenging — you can speak, but you choose not to. If you're chatting easily, go harder.

---

## BUILD-2 — Extended Tempo (3 Blocks)
**Weeks 11, 13 · Duration: 80 min**

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3
  ▸  4 min  ──  Easy Z1 (short recovery — legs should still feel it)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3
  ▸  4 min  ──  Easy Z1
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3  ← 30 min total tempo this session
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Cool-down Z1–Z2
```

> ⚠️ If HR spikes into Z4 in the third block, back off slightly — you want Z3 sustained, not a sprint.

---

## BUILD-3 — Sweet Spot
**Weeks 14, 15 · Duration: 85 min**

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2
  ▸ 15 min  ──  🟨🟧 SWEET SPOT (upper Z3 / lower Z4)
                    Most race-specific intensity in the plan
  ▸  5 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2
  ▸ 15 min  ──  🟨🟧 SWEET SPOT
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1–Z2
```

**Sweet spot = 88–93% FTP / HR at upper Z3.**

This is THE most important training zone for 70.3 cycling. It's the effort you'll hold for most of the 90km bike. 30 min at this intensity trains your body to sustain it for nearly 3 hours.

---

## BUILD-4 — Recovery Spin
**Weeks 12, 16 · Duration: 50 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1
  ▸ 30 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2 (easy gear, deliberate rest)
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down
```

# 🔴 PHASE 3: PEAK WORKOUTS
## Weeks 17–22 · The Summit

> *"The only way out is through."*

These are the hardest sessions of the 26-week plan. They will make November 1st feel manageable.

---

## PEAK-1 — Over-Unders
**Week 17 · Duration: 90 min**

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2
  
  ── REPEAT 3 TIMES ──────────────────────────────────
  │  6 min  ──  🟨 UNDER @ Z3 (below threshold)       │
  │  3 min  ──  🟧 OVER @ Z4 (above threshold — hard)  │
  │  3 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2 (partial recovery)          │
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Cool-down Z1
```

**Why over-unders?** At km 60–80 of the Kenting bike course, you'll hit fatigue and possibly a climb. This trains you to push through accumulated lactate — exactly what separates athletes who hit sub-6 from those who blow up.

The Z4 efforts should be **genuinely hard.** If they feel comfortable, go harder.

---

## PEAK-2 — Threshold Repeats
**Weeks 18, 19 · Duration: 90 min**
⚠️ *Hardest session in the entire plan.*

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2

  ▸ 20 min  ──  🟧 THRESHOLD @ Z3–Z4
                    Hard but sustainable. Focus on even effort.
                    Don't go out too hard in first 5 min.
  
  ▸  5 min  ──  Easy Z1 (full recovery — you earned it)
  
  ▸ 20 min  ──  🟧 THRESHOLD @ Z3–Z4
                    Same effort as first block. Stay disciplined.
                    ← 40 min total at threshold today
  
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1–Z2
```

> 🏆 **If you complete both PEAK-2 sessions, you have the bike fitness to hit sub-6.**

---

## PEAK-3 — Race Pace Maintenance
**Week 20 (Race Sim Week) · Duration: 50 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2
  ▸ 30 min  ──  🟨 RACE PACE @ Z3 (controlled, sustainable)
                    This is what 90km on race day should feel like.
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1
```

Recovery week — don't push. This session is about maintaining feel, not building fitness.

---

## PEAK-4 — Strength + Tempo
**Week 21 · Duration: 85 min**

```
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Warm-up Z1 → Z2

  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3 (normal cadence — 88–92 rpm)
  ▸  5 min  ──  Easy Z1
  
  ▸ 10 min  ──  💪 LOW CADENCE STRENGTH @ Z3 (70–75 rpm, harder gear)
                    Builds raw leg strength — crucial for Kenting hills
  ▸  5 min  ──  Easy Z1
  
  ▸ 10 min  ──  🟨 TEMPO @ Z3 (normal cadence — legs will be tired)
  
  ▸ 15 min  ──  Cool-down Z1–Z2
```

---

## PEAK-5 — Easy Spin (Taper begins)
**Week 22 · Duration: 75 min**

```
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Warm-up Z1
  ▸ 55 min  ──  Easy Z1–Z2. Enjoy it. The hard work is done.
  ▸ 10 min  ──  Cool-down Z1
```

# 🌡 LONG RIDE INDOORS — Saturday Option

When outdoor riding isn't possible, adapt Saturday's long ride:

| Planned Outdoor | Indoor Equivalent | Why shorter? |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| 90 min | 75 min | No coasting — every min is work |
| 2:00 | 1:45 | |
| 2:30 | 2:10 | |
| 3:00 | 2:30–2:40 | |

**Long indoor ride survival tips:**
- Change hand position every 20–30 min
- Keep a powerful fan on you — you WILL overheat
- Watch a race film, listen to a podcast, or use Zwift for mental engagement
- Keep nutrition within arm's reach
