# SWIM STRATEGY

**You have 3 swim sessions per week — Monday morning, Friday morning, and Sunday (short, poolside during kids coaching).** The Mon and Fri sessions are your primary quality swims. Sunday is a bonus easy/technique session — keep it short and never hard.

**Session focus by phase:**
- **Weeks 1–8 (Base):** Technique and efficiency. Bilateral breathing. Smooth tumble turns. Consistent sets.
- **Weeks 9–16 (Build):** Add interval sets. Begin open water practice on Fridays when accessible. Threshold sets from Week 13.
- **Weeks 17–22 (Peak):** Open water on Mondays. Race-pace sets on Fridays. Wetsuit practice from Week 14.
- **Race morning:** Stay calm at the swim start. Seed yourself honestly. The first 300m will be chaotic — stay wide if needed.

> **Kenting tip:** Ocean swim — expect some chop and current. Practice sighting a fixed object every 8–10 strokes. November water temp is typically 26–27°C — wetsuit may be optional or prohibited. Check race rules close to race day.

# BIKE STRATEGY

### Race Day Execution
```
  Km 0–15   → Settle in. Hold back. HR stays Z2–Z3.
  Km 15–60  → Race pace. 31–33 km/h. Fuel every 20–25 min.
              Drink every 15–20 min.
  Km 60–80  → This is where most people blow up.
              Stay disciplined. Don't chase others.
  Km 80–90  → Push. You've earned it. Run is coming.
```

> **Kenting terrain:** Rolling hills with some climbs. Not flat. Practice hill climbing from Phase 2 onwards outdoors when possible. Stand up on steep climbs to preserve legs.

# RUN STRATEGY

Your current base (20–40 km/week) is solid. Converting it into 70.3 run pacing is the job of Phases 2 and 3.

**Target half marathon splits:**
```
  Km 0–5    ──  6:10–6:20/km  (conservative, legs adjusting)
  Km 5–15   ──  5:55–6:00/km  (race pace, locked in)
  Km 15–21  ──  5:45–5:55/km  (if feeling good, push)
```

> **Heat warning — Kenting in November:** 28–32°C, humid. Build +10–15 min into your time targets vs cooler conditions. Pour cold water over yourself at EVERY aid station. Walk aid stations — costs 15 seconds and saves your core temp.

# RACE DAY NUTRITION PLAN

### Night Before
- Pasta or rice with lean protein. No heavy sauces. No new foods.
- Drink 2–3 litres of water throughout the day

### Race Morning (3 hrs before start)
- Rice cakes / banana + peanut butter / oats with honey
- Coffee if you normally drink it — no experiments
- 500ml water with electrolytes

### Swim — Nothing. Go.

### Bike (~2:48 on course)
```
  Every 20–25 min  ──  1 gel or half bar  +  water sip
  Every 30 min     ──  300–400ml fluid (electrolyte or water)
  Target           ──  60–80g carbs/hour (~280 cal/hr)
  2 full bottles + aid station water
```

### Run (~2:07 on course)
```
  Every 30–35 min  ──  1 gel (2–3 total)
  Every aid station ── Water or cola
  Walk aid stations    (saves core temp in Kenting heat)
```

> **Test your entire nutrition strategy in the Race Simulation (Week 20).** Race day is not the time to discover your stomach hates a particular gel.

### Kenting Heat Protocol
- Pre-race: ice towel on neck + ice in jersey pocket at T2
- Add sodium: electrolyte tablets, not just water (hyponatremia risk in heat)
- Monitor urine colour on race morning — should be pale yellow
